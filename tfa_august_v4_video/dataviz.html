dataviz the workshop

you are data, music is data, everything is data

The workshop prepared by our teachers was all about data. How to represent data in the way it makes sense. By showing it on a poster and a website at the same time.

a fruitfull brainstorm

ideas bursting out of our heads

After a personal reflexion, we shared our ideas and the following subjects came out

  • The influence of alcohol on the human relations ?
  • The analyse of the circumstances surrounding the death of well-known people ?
  • The comparison of foreign artists and the Belgian ones in our festivals ?
  • The count of the different countries present at Tomorrowland ?
  • The impact of the events through the medias and the social web ?
  • The statistics about some data of our school ?
  • The influence of the scales in music ?
  • The analyse of two different songs in relation to the instruments, the rythm and the tone ?

Four hours later, we retained the three latest subjects to start searching for databases.

The searching process

a long and tedious one

Our first task was to find a rich and a meaningful database. Since we were not finding any viable information about music, we tried something else. We created a form to be filled in by the students of our school. This table was a fail-safe in case we did not find the data we were looking for.

Thank goodness

finally we found a database

After two days of exploration on the web we finally found the support of our project : a big database containing 17000 songs with a ton of information about each of them. We needed to roam around the database to find interesting correlation. We found four different ones :
