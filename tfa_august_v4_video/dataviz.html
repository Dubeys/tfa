dataviz the workshop

you are data, music is data, everything is data

The workshop prepared by our teachers was all about data. How to represent data in the way it makes sense. By showing it on a poster and a website at the same time.

a fruitfull brainstorm

ideas bursting out of our heads

After a personal reflexion, we shared our ideas and the following subjects came out

  • The influence of alcohol on the human relations ?
  • The analyse of the circumstances surrounding the death of well-known people ?
  • The comparison of foreign artists and the Belgian ones in our festivals ?
  • The count of the different countries present at Tomorrowland ?
  • The impact of the events through the medias and the social web ?
  • The statistics about some data of our school ?
  • The influence of the scales in music ?
  • The analyse of two different songs in relation to the instruments, the rythm and the tone ?

Four hours later, we retained the three latest subjects to start searching for databases.

The searching process

a long and tedious one

Our first task was to find a rich and a meaningful database. Since we were not finding any viable information about music, we tried something else. We created a form to be filled in by the students of our school. This table was a fail-safe in case we did not find the data we were looking for.

Thank goodness

finally we found a database

After two days of exploration on the web we finally found the support of our project : a big database containing 17000 songs with a ton of information about each of them. We needed to roam around the database to find interesting correlations. We found four different ones :

Visual identity

because data visualisation has visual in it

We had the content, but no idea of how to represent it. So we started focusing on the visual part of our work. We made a lot of diferent versions before we were happy.

Static side of the...

because websites are not the only way

First thing we designed was the poster, it seemed to be the most logical order. By creating the poster we we're as well thinking about how we were going to be able to generate it with code. Whit this in the back of our heads we created an intricate graph, that we new could be generated on the website. We chose blue for the digital aspect of todays music storage. As well the background is dark, so the graph will realy pop out and be right in the face.

Dynamic side of the...

coding with a new library, INTERESTING !

Since we already designed the graph on our poster, the logical thing to do next was to code it into a website. We used « d3.js », it was a steap learning curve, but we did it. Finaly we added two more simple graphs, because we still had two more corelations we found in the databass. The website is based on the same colors as the poster but the background has turned in a more flashy and bright blue.
